2. Executive Summary CONFIRMED

Elevator Pitch

Chronicles of the Sovereign Door is a premium, narrative-first mobile RPG that reinvents the harem genre by bridging two distinct realities: modern-day Japan and the fantasy realm of Argena. Players assume the role of Kaito Amane, a boy who gains cheat-level powers through a mysterious door in his grandfather's house, allowing him to travel freely between worlds. Tasked with balancing a high school life full of romance and a fantasy destiny of saving the world, players must unite 20 powerful heroines from both Earth and Argena. Every bond forged—whether with a school idol or an elven princess—unlocks new potential in Kaito's dual life.

Target Audience

Demographics
  • Primary: Males 18–35.
  • Geography: Global (NA, EU, SEA, JP).
  • Spend Profile: Mid-core gamers accustomed to Battle Passes and cosmetic skins.
Psychographics
  • The "Dual Life" Fantasist: Loves the idea of being secretly OP in the real world.
  • The "Shipper": Obsesses over character dynamics across worlds.
  • The "Completionist": Enjoys leveling up stats that affect both realities.

Core Desire: These players want the ultimate wish-fulfillment fantasy: transcending their mundane life while becoming a hero in another. They crave emotional validation from diverse, high-quality heroines.

The "modern world cheat" sub-genre is exploding in popularity (anime/manga). Sovereign Door captures this zeitgeist with high production values and deep narrative consequences.

3. Creative Vision & Tone CONFIRMED

Tone Manifesto

"A tale of two worlds, one heart, and the door that connects them."

The tone oscillates between the heartwarming slice-of-life comedy of Earth—where Kaito is oblivious to his own charm—and the high-stakes epic fantasy of Argena. The contrast is key. In Argena, he is a warrior king in the making; on Earth, he is a shy boy trying to pass math class. The romance is sweet, earned, and often humorous, grounded in Kaito's genuine kindness and low self-esteem.

While Argena brings danger and war, Earth brings the emotional stakes of secrets and relationships. The game treats both worlds with equal narrative weight.

Visual Aesthetic Guide

  • Primary Palette: Deep Void Navy (#0b0d17), Starlight Gold (#c5a059), Dried Blood Crimson (#a62639).
  • UI Style: "Dimensional Rift." A blend of modern sleek glass interfaces (Earth menu) and ancient runic stone textures (Argena menu). The transition between styles should be seamless.
  • Character Art: High-fidelity 2D anime style. Distinct visual languages for Earth (school uniforms, modern fashion) and Argena (armor, robes).
  • Atmosphere: Magical particles drifting between realities. Contrast between the sterile brightness of the classroom and the lush, dangerous vibrancy of the fantasy forest.

Identity Matrix

What This Game IS What This Game is NOT
A story about self-acceptance through power. A story about bullying the weak.
A dual-life simulation. A generic fantasy-only isekai.
Romance driven by kindness and protection. Romance driven by arrogance or conquest.
Tactical combat enhanced by cheat skills. A mindless auto-battler.

Emotional Design Goals

  1. Empowerment: The player feels the rush of going from zero to hero, physically and socially.
  2. Connection: The player feels deeply attached to the heroines who see Kaito's true worth.
  3. Wonder: The discovery of the Other World feels magical and limitless.
  4. Balance: Managing the secret of the dual life creates exciting narrative tension.

4. Gameplay Pillars CONFIRMED

⚔️ Two Worlds, One Hero

Philosophy: The game loops between Earth (School Life) and Argena (Adventure). Progression in one world benefits the other. Items found in Argena can be used on Earth (and vice versa).

Violation: Making one world feel like filler or irrelevant to progression.

🔗 Bonds Are Power

Philosophy: A heroine's affection level directly dictates her combat efficiency. High affection unlocks "Resonance" skills. Kaito's "Holy King Authority" amplifies the power of those he trusts.

Violation: A relationship system that is entirely separate from the combat stats.

♟️ Cheat Skill Strategy

Philosophy: Combat is turn-based and strategic. Kaito has "Cheat Skills" (Absolute Defense, All-Weapon Mastery) that feel powerful but require resource management (Spiritual Power).

Violation: Making the MC so overpowered that gameplay becomes boring/trivial.

🎭 Kindness is Strength

Philosophy: Kaito's defining trait is his kindness. Narrative choices that reflect empathy and protection yield the best rewards and deepest bonds.

Violation: Rewarding cruel or selfish choices with "good" outcomes.

🌍 The Secret Life

Philosophy: Maintaining the masquerade is part of the fun. Using Argena skills on Earth to solve problems (without getting caught) is a key narrative mechanic.

Violation: Everyone on Earth finding out about magic immediately with no consequence.

5. Game Overview IN REVIEW

The Core Loop

  1. Earth Phase (School/Daily Life): Attend Seiran Academy, interact with Earth heroines, manage social stats, deal with modern problems using cheat skills.
  2. Sanctuary Phase (The House): The hub connecting worlds. Sort inventory, craft items, cook meals (buffs), interact with familiars (Night, Akatsuki).
  3. Argena Phase (Adventure): Step through the Door. Explore dungeons, fight monsters, complete quests for the Guild or Kingdoms.
  4. Level Up Phase: Defeating monsters yields XP and drops. Stats increase massively. Skills evolve. Return to Earth stronger than before.

Session Flow (15 Minutes)

  • Min 0-2: Login at the Sanctuary. Collect familiar gifts. Check Earth/Argena calendar.
  • Min 2-10: Play one Main Story chapter (Reading + 1 Combat encounter in either world).
  • Min 10-15: Manage gear, upgrade one skill, set up "Sanctuary" farming/crafting.

Game Modes

  • Chronicles (Story Mode): The main 8-Arc saga spanning both worlds.
  • The Great Demon's Den (Dungeon): Procedurally generated dungeon crawling in the forest near the house.
  • School Festival (Social): Earth-based minigames and relationship events.
  • World Boss (Raid): Defeating massive threats (e.g., Evil Avatars) with other players' support units.

Backend Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth), Node.js (Game Logic API), Edge Functions.

6. The Protagonist: Kaito Amane CONFIRMED

Backstory & Reincarnation

Pre-Transformation: Kaito was a bullied, overweight boy with low self-esteem, abandoned by his parents and raised by his kind grandfather, Hiroshi. After Hiroshi's death, Kaito was left alone in the house, facing eviction and school torment.

The Door: In a fit of frustration, he punched a wall, revealing a secret room containing a mysterious Door and alien weaponry. Stepping through, he killed a high-level Bloody Ogre. The resulting level-up instantly reshaped his body into a godlike, handsome form and granted him immense power.

Personality Dimensions

  • Outwardly: Incredibly handsome, athletic, and polite. A "perfect prince" to strangers.
  • Inwardly: Humble, anxious, and kind. Still sees himself as the "useless fatty" deep down. Oblivious to romance.
  • Voice: Gentle, hesitant, but firm when protecting others.

The "Cheat" Mechanic

Kaito possesses the "Master of the Door" trait. He can access a personal dimension (the House) and use skills from Argena on Earth.

Stat Description Gameplay Effect
SPIRIT (HP) Life force & Spiritual Power. Used for high-level magic and "Holy King" skills.
MIGHT (ATK) Physical strength. Determines damage with all weapons (Spear, Sword, Fists).
SPEED (AGI) Agility & Reflexes. Determines turn order and evasion chance.
MAGIC (INT) Magical aptitude. Determines spell potency and resistance.
LUCK Fate manipulation. Increases drop rates, crit chance, and "lucky" narrative outcomes.
AUTHORITY Holy King presence. Buffs all allies. Unlocks high-tier dialogue options.

Class Progression Tree

  • Tier 1: Survivor. Basic combat skills. Spear mastery.
  • Tier 2: Explorer. Magic mastery. Mapping. Analysis.
  • Tier 3: Lord. "Holy King" skills unlocked. Massive AoE buffs.
  • Tier 4: Sovereign. Master of space/time. Dual-world manipulation.

12. Combat & Conflict Design CONFIRMED

"Cheat Tactics" System

Turn-based 4v4. Fast-paced. Strategic.

Resonance Mechanic: Heroines with high affection for Kaito gain "Resonance" points each turn. At 100%, they can perform a "Dual Union" attack with Kaito.

Strategy: Use Earth heroines for buffs/debuffs and Argena heroines for raw damage.

Kaito's Commands

  1. Attack: Standard physical hit.
  2. Absolute Defense: Nullifies next attack on an ally.
  3. Heal: Restores HP using Spiritual Power.
  4. Magic: Casts learned spells (Fire, Water, etc.).
  5. HOLY KING AUTHORITY: Ultimate buff. Maximizes all ally stats for 3 turns.

Conflict Types

  • Monster Battle: Standard combat in Argena.
  • School Conflict: Non-lethal "Social Battles" on Earth.
  • Boss Raid: Multi-stage fights against "Evils" or "Legends."

20. Narrative Bible — Characters in Full CONFIRMED

Writer's Directive

Every heroine in Chronicles of the Sovereign Door is a complete person before Kaito arrives in her life. They have histories, scars, dreams, and deeply held beliefs. The romance does not define them — it completes them. Writers must always ask: "What does this heroine want that has nothing to do with Kaito?" Only then can they write the moment she chooses him.

🗡 The Protagonist — Kaito Amane

Core Identity

"The boy the world forgot — and the man that two worlds needed."

Kaito is not a power fantasy. He is a kindness fantasy. In a world that rewards beauty and strength, he was worthless by every metric — until the Door changed everything. But the transformation changed his face, not his soul. He is still the boy who took beatings rather than cry in front of others. The boy who cooked for a grandfather who was the only light in his darkness. He wants nothing grand: a meal cooked in peace, a morning without dread, and maybe — someday — someone who sees him before they see his face.

  • Speaking Voice: Gentle, measured, often trails off when flustered. Uses formal speech out of habit (learned from his grandfather). Goes quiet before he acts, not while thinking.
  • When Angry: Kaito does not shout. His voice drops. His eyes go cold. The contrast is terrifying. He has only truly been angry three times in the story, and each time the scene becomes a landmark moment.
  • Obliviousness: Kaito's inability to recognize romantic signals is not stupidity — it is self-protection. He genuinely cannot believe he is someone anyone would fall for. This makes every heroine's moment of honest confession a story of breaking through his armor, not just his schedule.
  • Cooking: His most intimate form of love. When Kaito cooks for someone, it means he trusts them. Natsume's arc begins here. His grandfather's lessons live in every meal.
  • Moral Core: He will not leave someone in danger. Not a hero, not a saint — just a boy who never learned how to walk past someone who needed help. This gets him into almost every arc's conflict.

🌸 EARTH HEROINES — Full Narrative Profiles

Airi Haruno

CORE Seiran Academy · Heroine #1

"The Academy Princess" — Support / Buffer — Romance Energy: Pure Devotion

Full Biography

Airi Haruno is the only daughter of Seiran Academy's chairman, Tsuyoshi Haruno. She grew up surrounded by privilege and expectation, learning early that people either feared her family's influence or tried to use it. Her mother was an elegant woman who prioritized grace over warmth, and her father, while well-meaning, was perpetually busy reshaping the next generation of Japan's elite. Airi learned to perform kindness because genuine kindness left her vulnerable. She became the Academy's "princess" — admired from a safe distance, approached with agendas, and perfectly, exhaustingly alone.

She is genuinely kind. That is not a performance. She is clumsy in sports with a supernatural degree of ineptitude — she once broke a school record for throwing a basketball backwards through a second-floor window — but she laughs at herself with genuine warmth. She joined the student council not for prestige, but because she wanted to do something real. She is the type who carries an umbrella for others and forgets her own.

Emotional Wound

Airi has never been chosen purely. Every friendship came with invisible strings: proximity to her father, invitations to school events, charity from popular girls who needed an "innocent" sidekick. The day she was harassed outside a convenience store and a strange, overweight, ordinary-looking boy stepped in front of her without hesitation — not because she was the chairman's daughter, not because she was beautiful, but simply because she was scared — was the first time in years someone acted without agenda. She memorized that face, even as it swelled from the beating he took for her.

When she found that same face transformed — godlike, impossible — she recognized him anyway. Not his face. His posture. The way he walked: forward-leaning, as if carrying invisible weight, but still forward.

Arc Progression

  • Stage 1 — Curiosity: After running a background check (she is her father's daughter), Airi approaches Kaito at his old school and invites him to transfer. She tells herself it's gratitude. It's not.
  • Stage 2 — Friendship: At Seiran, she becomes his first real guide. She covers his social fumbles with grace. He brings her lunch once — something his grandfather taught him. She cries in the bathroom afterwards and doesn't know why.
  • Stage 3 — Discovery: She accidentally follows Kaito through the Door. She sees Argena. She does not scream. She reaches for his hand. "You carry this alone," she says quietly. "You shouldn't have to."
  • Stage 4 — Crisis: Her father discovers Kaito's true nature through official channels. He offers Kaito a "deal." Airi must choose between her father's world and what she now knows is real.
  • Stage 5 — Resolution: She chooses Kaito — not out of romance, but out of conviction. The romance follows like sunrise: quiet, inevitable, warm.

Signature Scene (First Key CG)

Scene: "The Umbrella"

Rain outside the school gates. Airi stands with a borrowed umbrella — not hers. She sees Kaito soaked to the bone, staring up at the sky with an expression she cannot read. She holds the umbrella over both of them without a word. He looks down at her, startled. She looks up at him, not startled at all.

AIRI: "You really don't carry one, do you."

KAITO: "I forget."

AIRI: "I know. I've been watching." (beat) "Sorry, that was strange."

KAITO: "No. It wasn't." (pause) "Thank you for watching."

Jealousy Behavior

Airi's jealousy is the most controlled and therefore the most dangerous. She does not raise her voice. She becomes more helpful.
